Our Story

A pit, a recipe, and a whole lot of oak.

In September 1972, Robert and Babette Schexnaider opened Oak Grove Smoke House on Old Jefferson Highway with a simple idea: smoke meat the way South Louisiana families always had — slow, over oak, no shortcuts. Word got around fast. The andouille and tasso became staples in kitchens all over Ascension Parish.

When mail-order customers kept asking how to build a real roux or a proper jambalaya with our smoked meats, we did what any Cajun cook would: we bottled the know-how. In 1975 our Gumbo Base and Jambalaya mixes hit shelves across Louisiana, Texas, Arkansas, Mississippi, and Alabama — and they’ve been shipping ever since.

The Oak Grove Way

No shortcuts, no liquid smoke.

1

Season by hand

Every batch is ground and seasoned to the same recipe the Schexnaiders wrote down in ’72 — garlic, cayenne, black pepper, and patience.

2

Hang & smoke over oak

Links go up in the smokehouse over real oak, low and slow, until the color and snap are exactly right. No gas, no cheating.

3

Cut, weigh, hand it over

Fresh out of the smoke and into your ice chest — or vacuum-packed for the drive home and the freezer.
